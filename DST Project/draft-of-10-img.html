notes: We have to go back to all the way to 1784. where this gentleman on the left. {speak less brazenly} Who you might recognize as Benjamin Franklin. {return to normal present voice} Writes a letter to the Journal of Paris {sidenote voice} a scientific journal {return to normal} about how we could save money if we just woke up when the sun goes up and sleep near when the sun goes down. {questioning} Wait how is this DST? {answer tone} It isn't, but this does the core idea of using more daylight and less lights. But this other lad on the right? He is William Willett. The real person who created DST in its first form. He proposed to parliament a schedule of clock changes to advance the clocks a total of 80 minutes in the summer. This worked by shifting the clocks 20 minutes every Sunday at 2 am starting in April. and reverse starting in September. {side comment} I feel bad for the people who would have to stay up to change the clocks every Sunday night. {normal} But luckily for those people, Parliament thought it was to complicated and declined the idea. ---
